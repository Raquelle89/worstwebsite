Outkast (sometimes written as OutKast) were an American hip hop duo formed in 1992 in East
Point, Georgia, consisting of Atlanta-based rappers André "3000" Benjamin (formerly known
as Dré) and Antwan "Big Boi" Patton.[1] Widely recognized for their intricate lyricism,
memorable melodies and positive messages,[3] Outkast is often regarded as one of the
greatest and most influential hip hop acts of all time.[4][5][6][7] The duo achieved both
critical acclaim and commercial success from the mid-1990s to the early 2000s, helping to
popularize Southern hip hop[1] while experimenting with diverse genres such as funk,
psychedelia, jazz, and techno.
